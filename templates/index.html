DIRTY WATER

CO2 in air pollution significantly impacts water habitats. Increased atmospheric CO2 levels lead to ocean acidification, where CO2 dissolves in seawater forming carbonic acid. This acidification affects marine life, especially organisms with calcium carbonate shells, such as corals and some shellfish, making it harder for them to form and maintain their shells. Additionally, higher CO2 levels contribute to global warming, causing ocean temperatures to rise. Warmer waters disrupt marine ecosystems, affect species distribution, and lead to coral bleaching, which devastates coral reefs and the biodiversity they support. Overall, the increase in CO2 has profound and detrimental effects on water habitats, threatening the health and stability of aquatic ecosystems.
BUSY TRAFFIC

Heavy traffic is a major source of CO2 emissions. The exhaust from countless vehicles releases significant amounts of carbon dioxide into the atmosphere, contributing to air pollution and climate change. This increased CO2 not only affects air quality but also accelerates global warming, impacting both terrestrial and aquatic ecosystems. Rising temperatures and ocean acidification harm marine life, disrupt weather patterns, and threaten biodiversity. To mitigate these harmful emissions, it is essential to reduce traffic congestion, promote public transportation, and adopt cleaner, more sustainable transportation methods.
CLEAR SKY

Reducing CO2 emissions is crucial for obtaining clear skies and combating climate change. Lower CO2 levels lead to decreased air pollution, resulting in clearer, healthier skies. With fewer greenhouse gases trapping heat in the atmosphere, temperatures stabilize, reducing the frequency of extreme weather events like storms and hurricanes. Additionally, decreasing CO2 emissions slows down the process of global warming, preserving the delicate balance of ecosystems and protecting biodiversity. By prioritizing sustainable practices and transitioning to renewable energy sources, we can pave the way for clearer skies and a brighter, more sustainable future.
